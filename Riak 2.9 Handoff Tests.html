Brief History

pre-2003 - local spend increasing, limited integration

2003 - contract five local service providers (LSPs) and national services (NASPs)

2006 - existing providers fill gaps from LSPs

.... LSPs suffer on appetite for change management, and technology delays

2012 - expensive success in NASPs, costly failure in LSPs

2016 - bespoke NASPs move in-house, commodity NASPs re-contracted, LSPs wound down

Brief History - Benefit Realisation

2012 National Audit Office review - expected benefit realised

Commodity services (e.g. network, mail, PACS) - 80% to 95%

Bespoke national infrastructure (i.e. NASPs) - 69% to 72%

Change programmes enabled by NASPs - 2% to 47%

....Note that volume on Spine has nearly trebled since 2012

Bespoke local services (i.e. LSPs) - 2% to 14%

NASP Success Story - Spine

Split between:

  • Core - the big volume heart of the Spine

  • Identity - authentication, authorisation of professionals

  • Secondary Use - focused on tariffing for secondary care

NASP Success Story - Spine Core

Services supported:

  • Electronic Prescriptions (Primary Care)

  • Demographics

  • Summary Care Record

  • Consent and subject access alerting, reporting and analysis

  • GP2GP, eReferral, Pathology messaging

Spine Core - The In-House Solution

Took 100 man years from inception to 1-years service

Requires just over 100 commodity 1RU servers in live

Release costs are < 0.1% of previous release costs

90% reduction in operating costs

Total running team of 35 people supporting and ...

... Managing more than £10m pa of change backlog

Spine Core - The In-House Solution

(Nearly) like-for-like functional replacement ...

99.999% available since go live

Supports over 300 message interactions, five UI applications

43M messages a day

Provides accesss to 2bn records and documents

The NHS waits more than 1,000 working days less each day

Spine Core - The Change in Approach

Technology - learn the lessons of Google etc

Contractual - accept risk, make incentives natural

Management - embrace Agile/LEAN ethos

Operations - a consistent response to failure

Delivery - automation everywhere

People - focus on value, not unit cost

Architecture - continuous improvement in simplicity

Spine Core - The Change in Approach

Bending on the previous principles has led to reduced success

  • Identity Services - operational cost, opportunity cost
  • eReferrals - go-live stability, operational cost

There exists continuous pressure to bend on principles
